Respect the chapters, not just the cover.
A seed is small, but holds a tree,
A child may dream, and so shall be.
Their voices light, yet sharp and true,
Their ideas fresh, their wisdom new.

An adult's path may seem unclear,
But time's not wasted, nor to fear.
A spark can rise when least they know,
And brilliance waits for chance to show.

The aged hands that shake with grace,
Have built the world, each line a trace.
Their strength lives on, though time may wear,
Their stories speak—so stop, and care.

We are all pages in time's great book,
Each age a chapter, take a look.
For young, for old, for those unseen,
Respect the story yet between.
A child may stumble, trip, and fall,
But don't mistake them weak or small.
Their minds are stars, alive and bright,
Unshaped by fear, alight with might.

The questions they ask, the dreams they chase,
Are seeds of change, a future's place.
Don't silence their thoughts, don't call them naïve,
For the boldest wonders are born when they believe.
The middle years, a silent climb,
A dance with doubt, a race with time.
The world may ask, "What have you done?"
But not all battles end in sun.

Some paths are long, some sparks take time,
Not every hill is meant to climb.
Do not dismiss the quiet fight—
The unseen work that builds the light.
These wrinkled hands, they shaped the earth,
They've known both sorrow and of mirth.
Beneath the grey, the years are stored,
Each line a story, each heart adored.

Don't call them weak, don't look away,
Their wisdom speaks of yesterday.
A bridge they are from past to now,
So listen close, and learn to bow.
