Bright Night

A bright light, a sunny day,
A starry glow in twilight's bay.
Some adore the day's embrace,
Others enjoy the nightlight's grace.

Choices made by hearts, we see,
Who to love, and who to be.
Kindness shared or cold disdain,
Paths we make, joy or pain.

Maybe beyond this world we know,
A bright night shines, a peaceful glow,
Where love transcends all hue or rite,
No black or white, the purest sight.

No yellow, purple, none divide,
Only hearts standing side by side.
A bond untouched by hate or hands,
United souls in boundless lands.

No heavy gate of grief or hate,
The darkness fades, and hope takes flight,
A brighter world, a shining night,
Where chains of past no longer bind.

Maybe beyond, where skies expand,
A world released from chains and sand.
No silent fears, no judgment’s weight,
No heavy gate of grief or hate.

But here below, the world still stings,
With pointed words and broken wings.
Skin like soil, or shades of night,
Still blinds some hearts from others' light.

Fingers rise to place the blame,
While voices drown the silent names.
I still dream this truth can rise,
Beyond the dark, where hope survives.

Maybe beyond, there’s hope anew,
A bright night sky for me and you.
Where peace and love, forever near,
Can light the world, untouched by fear.

By: Michael Hans G. Magbojos | BSIT-2a

Music: Phyrnna - A Stroll Through Nostalgia
